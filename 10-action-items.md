# Action Items

Prioritized list of recommended next steps, organized by urgency and potential impact.

---

## 1. CRITICAL: Verify QSBS Eligibility

**Why:** QSBS (IRC Section 1202) is Gumroad's most valuable tax asset. Sahil's exclusion alone could save $2.38M. But the eligibility depends on verifiable facts -- particularly the $50M gross assets test -- that must be confirmed with actual financial data.

**Time-sensitive:** The 7,387 crowdfunding investors from March 2021 are approaching (or have reached) the 5-year QSBS holding threshold. If their Crowd SAFEs have converted or will convert, the QSBS analysis is immediately relevant to thousands of people.

**Actions:**
- Obtain Gumroad balance sheets from each date stock was issued (2011, 2012, 2021, and any option exercise dates)
- Confirm aggregate adjusted basis of corporate assets did not exceed $50M at any relevant issuance date
- Analyze Crowd SAFE terms to determine when the QSBS holding period begins for crowdfunding investors
- Consult securities counsel on investor communication obligations regarding QSBS
- Document and preserve all QSBS eligibility evidence

---

## 2. CRITICAL: Document Business Purpose for Retained Earnings

**Why:** With $8.9M in net income and only $5.34M distributed, the ~$3.56M retained in the corporation is exposed to the accumulated earnings tax (20% surtax under IRC Section 531) unless Gumroad can demonstrate specific, definite, and feasible business purposes for the retention.

**Actions:**
- Draft board resolutions identifying specific uses for retained capital (R&D investment, working capital reserves, potential acquisitions, product development roadmap)
- Attach dollar amounts and timelines to each identified purpose
- Ensure board minutes reflect discussion and deliberation of retention decisions
- Review annually and update as purposes are fulfilled or changed
- Consider engaging a valuation professional if significant capital is being earmarked for specific projects

---

## 3. HIGH: Conduct R&D Tax Credit Study

**Why:** Gumroad's software development and AI integration work likely qualifies for the federal R&D tax credit. This is a dollar-for-dollar reduction in tax liability -- not a deduction. Estimated annual value: $65,000 to $130,000+.

**Actions:**
- Engage a qualified R&D credit specialist to conduct a formal study
- Identify qualifying activities (software development, AI/ML integration, technical problem-solving)
- Document qualifying expenditures (contractor costs for R&D, cloud computing for R&D)
- Evaluate retroactive claims for open tax years (generally 3 years back)
- Implement ongoing documentation practices (time tracking by activity, project logs)

---

## 4. HIGH: Optimize Sahil's Multi-Source Income

**Why:** Sahil has an unusually complex income picture: IRS W-2 wages, Gumroad dividends, and potentially Gumroad compensation. These streams interact in ways that affect marginal tax rates, FICA calculations, NIIT thresholds, and estimated payment requirements.

**Actions:**
- Model combined income from all sources for 2026 tax year
- Optimize the split between Gumroad salary and dividends
- Coordinate W-4 withholding at the IRS with estimated payments on investment income
- Evaluate whether additional Gumroad compensation (beyond dividends) makes sense
- Review state tax obligations across all income sources

---

## 5. HIGH: Audit Contractor Classification Compliance

**Why:** Twenty-five independent contractors working 20-35 hours per week presents significant reclassification risk. The IRS and state agencies have been increasingly aggressive about worker classification. Reclassification can result in back taxes, penalties, and interest for multiple years.

**Actions:**
- Review all contractor agreements for proper classification language
- Assess each contractor relationship against IRS common law factors and applicable state tests
- Identify highest-risk relationships (long tenure, high hours, core business functions)
- Consider converting the most at-risk contractors to employees
- Evaluate the Voluntary Classification Settlement Program (VCSP) if risk is significant
- Implement annual classification reviews going forward

---

## 6. MEDIUM: Evaluate Retirement Plan Options

**Why:** A combined defined benefit plan and 401(k) could shelter $250,000 to $400,000 per year in tax-deductible contributions, with Sahil as the primary beneficiary. This redirects money from tax payments into retirement savings.

**Actions:**
- Engage a retirement plan actuary to model defined benefit plan options
- Design a 401(k) with employer match/profit-sharing component
- Evaluate nondiscrimination testing implications (especially if any contractors are reclassified as employees)
- Compare plan administration costs against tax savings
- Implement for the 2026 plan year if analysis is favorable

---

## 7. MEDIUM: Review Dividend Payout Ratio

**Why:** The 60% charter requirement creates a structured distribution policy, but the optimal payout ratio from a tax perspective may differ. Retaining more income is taxed at 21% (corporate level); distributing it adds 23.8% (qualified dividend level). But retaining too much triggers accumulated earnings tax risk.

**Actions:**
- Model the tax impact of different payout ratios (60% vs. 70% vs. 50%)
- Evaluate whether the 60% charter provision is optimally set
- Coordinate payout analysis with accumulated earnings tax defense
- Consider whether charter amendment is appropriate (if a different ratio is significantly better)

---

## 8. MEDIUM: Assess IP Valuation from Open-Sourcing

**Why:** Gumroad's open-source contributions have potential tax implications -- charitable deduction opportunities, R&D credit qualification for underlying development work, and support for the accumulated earnings tax defense narrative.

**Actions:**
- Inventory all open-sourced intellectual property
- Determine Gumroad's tax basis in contributed IP
- Evaluate whether any charitable deduction is available under IRC Section 170
- Document the business purpose of open-source strategy (community building, talent acquisition, competitive positioning)
- Cross-reference with R&D credit study for qualifying development activities

---

## 9. LOW: Model Crowd SAFE Conversion Timeline

**Why:** The 7,387 unconverted Crowd SAFEs represent a significant contingent liability and structural factor. Understanding when and how they might convert affects QSBS analysis, cap table planning, dividend mechanics, and future financing or exit scenarios.

**Actions:**
- Review SAFE terms to identify all conversion triggers
- Model the dilution impact of conversion at various valuations
- Evaluate whether proactive conversion (vs. waiting for a trigger) is advantageous
- Coordinate with securities counsel on conversion mechanics and disclosure requirements

---

## 10. LOW: Estate Planning at $100M+ Valuation

**Why:** With Gumroad valued at $100M+ and Sahil holding a majority stake, estate and gift tax planning is relevant even though he is in his early 30s. The federal estate tax exemption is currently $13.99M per person (2025), but is scheduled to revert to approximately $7M (adjusted) after 2025 under current law.

**Actions:**
- Evaluate Sahil's current estate plan (or need for one)
- Model estate tax exposure at various exit valuations
- Consider lifetime gifting strategies using valuation discounts
- Evaluate irrevocable trust structures (GRATs, SLATs, etc.)
- Coordinate with QSBS planning (gifted stock may carry QSBS eligibility to recipients)
