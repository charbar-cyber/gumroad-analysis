## About Charlie

I became a CPA and a Certified Fraud Examiner. I worked at firms of various sizes, learning how the industry operates at scale. The pattern was always the same: smart people doing repetitive work that a machine should be doing, bottlenecked by software built in the 1990s, charging by the hour for tasks that should take minutes.

Then AI changed everything.

Not in the abstract, futuristic way people talk about it on Twitter. In the practical, immediate way: I could suddenly do in 20 minutes what used to take a senior associate four hours. Tax research that required digging through CCH or RIA for an afternoon could be synthesized in seconds. First drafts of memos, returns, analyses -- work that was 80% mechanical and 20% judgment -- could be automated, with the CPA focusing entirely on the 20% that actually requires professional expertise.

I left to go all-in. I bounced around a few firms, testing the idea, refining the workflows. Then I started Barmore CPA as an AI-native firm from day one.

## How We Work

Everything we do is built on AI-powered workflows. Not AI as a gimmick or a marketing angle -- AI as the core operating infrastructure.

**What that means in practice:**

- We do not have a bullpen of staff accountants manually keying in data. AI handles data extraction, categorization, and initial analysis.
- Tax research is AI-assisted with human verification. We get to the right answer faster, with more comprehensive citation, than a traditional research process.
- Client deliverables are drafted by AI and reviewed by a CPA. The CPA's job is judgment, quality control, and client communication -- not formatting spreadsheets.
- Real-time advisory is possible because our systems can process information as it arrives, not in batch during tax season.

**What that does NOT mean:**

- We do not hand clients AI-generated output and call it done. Every deliverable is reviewed by a licensed CPA.
- We do not skip the hard thinking. AI is not good at judgment. It is good at gathering, organizing, and drafting. The analysis is mine.
- We do not cut corners on compliance. The same professional standards apply regardless of how the work is produced.

## The Live 1040 Vision

Here is where we are going.

Imagine a tax return that is not a once-a-year document prepared in March from a shoebox of receipts. Instead, it is a living model -- an AI agent connected to your bank accounts, brokerage, payroll, and financial systems, continuously mapping transactions to the right lines of your 1040.

Not at year-end. In real time.

The system sees a large capital gain in your brokerage account and immediately calculates the tax impact, checks for loss harvesting opportunities in other positions, and alerts you before December 31. It notices your W-2 withholding is tracking below your projected liability and suggests a W-4 adjustment. It identifies a retirement contribution opportunity that would reduce your effective rate by 2 points and models the impact.

This is not science fiction. The components exist today -- bank feeds, API integrations, tax calculation engines, AI reasoning. The missing piece is a CPA firm that is willing to architect the system and stand behind the output with professional judgment.

I am currently building this for my own return as the prototype. My 1040 is the test case. When it works -- when I can point to a full year of real-time tax optimization, with every position documented and defensible -- we will roll it out to clients.

## The CPA Role Evolves

The traditional CPA firm model is:
1. Client sends documents once a year
2. Staff prepares the return
3. Manager reviews
4. Partner signs
5. Client gets a bill and a PDF

That model is not wrong. It is just slow, reactive, and expensive relative to what is now possible.

The new model is:
1. Systems continuously ingest financial data
2. AI processes, categorizes, and maps to tax positions
3. CPA reviews, applies judgment, and advises proactively
4. Client receives ongoing optimization, not just annual compliance

In this model, the CPA is an architect and reviewer of AI-driven advisory. The volume of work goes up (because you are monitoring continuously, not annually), but the marginal cost per insight goes down dramatically. That is how you build a $10M firm with two people.
