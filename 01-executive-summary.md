# Executive Summary

This analysis reviews Gumroad's corporate structure, tax position, and optimization opportunities. The findings fall into three categories: critical issues requiring immediate attention, warnings that need monitoring, and opportunities worth pursuing.

---

## CRITICAL

### S-Corp Conversion Is Almost Certainly Impossible

Gumroad fails at least four of the five S-Corp eligibility requirements under IRC Section 1361:

- **More than 100 shareholders.** Gumroad has 7,387+ crowdfunding investors from the March 2021 Republic raise alone, plus VCs, angels, and team members. The S-Corp limit is 100.
- **Multiple classes of stock.** Remaining Series A investors likely hold preferred stock with ~$2.5M in liquidation preferences. S-Corps may only have one class of stock.
- **Non-resident alien shareholders.** With 7,387 investors from a public crowdfunding campaign, it is statistically near-certain that some are non-resident aliens. S-Corps cannot have NRA shareholders.
- **Corporate and partnership shareholders.** Accel Partners, First Round Capital, and Collaborative Fund are typically structured as partnerships. S-Corps cannot have entity shareholders (with limited exceptions for certain trusts and estates).

The unconverted Crowd SAFEs add another layer of complexity. Any restructuring attempt (F reorganization, buyback program, SAFE cancellation) would be prohibitively expensive, legally complex, and potentially destructive to shareholder value.

**This is actually good news.** Read on.

### Accumulated Earnings Tax Risk (IRC Section 531)

Gumroad reported approximately $8.9M in net income for 2023 but distributed only $5.34M (60%) as dividends. The retained earnings need a documented, reasonable business purpose -- otherwise the IRS can impose a 20% surtax on the accumulated amount under IRC Sections 531-537.

This is not a theoretical risk. Gumroad is a highly profitable C-Corp with a lean operating model and limited capital expenditure needs. That profile attracts scrutiny.

**Action required:** Document the business purpose for all retained earnings (growth capital, working capital reserves, R&D investment, acquisition fund, etc.) and maintain board minutes supporting these decisions.

### QSBS Is the Crown Jewel -- Do NOT Convert to S-Corp

Section 1202 of the Internal Revenue Code -- Qualified Small Business Stock -- is the single most valuable tax asset in Gumroad's structure. It allows 100% exclusion of capital gains on qualified stock held for more than 5 years, up to $10M per shareholder (or 10x basis, whichever is greater).

Sahil's founder stock from 2011 almost certainly qualifies. On a future exit:

- **Potential tax savings: up to $2.38M** ($10M exclusion at 23.8% combined federal capital gains + NIIT rate)
- His stock has been held for 15 years -- well past the 5-year minimum
- Gumroad appears to meet the qualified trade or business and active business requirements
- The $50M gross assets test needs verification but was likely met at issuance

**Converting to S-Corp would permanently and irrevocably destroy QSBS eligibility for all shareholders.** This is the single strongest argument for maintaining C-Corp status. Gumroad should never convert.

## WARNING

### Multi-Source Income Coordination

Sahil's tax situation is unusually complex. He has W-2 income from the IRS (career employee since November 2025), dividend income from Gumroad, and potentially compensation from Gumroad as well. These income streams interact in ways that affect marginal rates, FICA obligations, net investment income tax thresholds, and estimated payment requirements.

Without coordinated planning across all sources, he is likely overpaying or underpaying in ways that create either unnecessary tax liability or penalty exposure.

### Crowdfunding Investors Hitting QSBS Threshold NOW

The 7,387 investors from the March 2021 Republic crowdfunding round are reaching their 5-year QSBS holding period right now -- March 2026. If their Crowd SAFEs have converted (or convert) to equity, and if Gumroad meets all QSBS requirements, these investors may be sitting on significant tax-free gain potential.

This is time-sensitive. Communication and verification should happen immediately.

### Contractor Classification Risk

Gumroad operates with approximately 25 "independent contractors" working 20-35 hours per week. The IRS and state agencies have been increasingly aggressive about reclassification of workers who exhibit employee-like characteristics.

Key risk factors: regular hours, ongoing relationships, work integrated into core business operations, and potential economic dependence on Gumroad. Misclassification can result in back taxes, penalties, and interest for unpaid employment taxes going back multiple years.

## OPPORTUNITY

### R&D Tax Credits

Gumroad's software development and AI integration work likely qualifies for the federal Research and Development tax credit under IRC Section 41. Based on estimated qualifying expenditures, the annual credit potential is **$65,000 to $130,000+**.

For a C-Corp, this is a dollar-for-dollar reduction in tax liability -- not a deduction, a credit.

### Competitive Effective Tax Rate

With proper C-Corp optimization (qualified dividend treatment, retirement plan contributions, R&D credits, and strategic compensation), Gumroad's blended effective tax rate can reach approximately **26-28%**. This is competitive with -- and in some scenarios better than -- what an S-Corp pass-through structure would achieve, especially when accounting for self-employment taxes and state tax considerations.

The "double taxation" problem of C-Corps is real but dramatically overstated when proper planning is applied.

### Retirement Plan Strategies

As a C-Corp, Gumroad can sponsor retirement plans that create significant tax deductions. A combined defined benefit plan and 401(k) structure could shelter **$250,000 to $400,000 per year** in tax-deductible contributions, with Sahil as the primary beneficiary.

This money is not lost -- it is redirected from the IRS into Sahil's retirement accounts, growing tax-deferred.
