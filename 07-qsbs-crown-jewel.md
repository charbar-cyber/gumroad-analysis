# QSBS: The Crown Jewel

If there is one takeaway from this entire analysis, it is this: **Gumroad's C-Corp status is not a tax problem. It is a tax asset.** And QSBS -- Qualified Small Business Stock under IRC Section 1202 -- is the reason.

QSBS is the single most generous capital gains provision in the Internal Revenue Code. For Gumroad's shareholders, it could mean millions of dollars in tax-free gains on a future exit.

Converting to an S-Corp would permanently and irrevocably destroy this benefit.

---

## What Is QSBS?

Section 1202 of the Internal Revenue Code allows shareholders of qualified small business stock to **exclude 100% of capital gains** from the sale of that stock, subject to limits, if certain requirements are met.

The key terms:

| Term | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Exclusion percentage** | 100% for stock acquired after September 27, 2010 |
| **Per-shareholder limit** | Greater of $10M or 10x basis (pre-OBBBA); $15M or 15x basis (post-OBBBA, July 2025) |
| **Minimum holding period** | 5 years |
| **Eligible entity** | Domestic C-Corporation |
| **Gross assets test** | Corporation had no more than $50M in gross assets at issuance (pre-OBBBA); $75M (post-OBBBA) |
| **Active business test** | At least 80% of assets used in active conduct of a qualified trade or business |
| **Qualified trade or business** | Excludes certain service businesses (health, law, engineering, finance, etc.), but technology/software is fully eligible |

### OBBBA Changes (Signed July 4, 2025)

The One Big Beautiful Bill Act significantly enhanced QSBS benefits:

| Parameter | Pre-OBBBA | Post-OBBBA |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| Exclusion cap | $10M or 10x basis | **$15M or 15x basis** |
| Gross assets threshold | $50M | **$75M** |
| Holding period (100%) | 5+ years | 5+ years (with new tiered option: 50% at 3yr, 75% at 4yr) |

These changes apply to qualifying stock and represent a major windfall for Gumroad shareholders.

The math on maximum benefit:

- $10M–$15M in excluded gains (depending on OBBBA applicability)
- At 20% federal long-term capital gains rate + 3.8% NIIT = 23.8%
- **Maximum tax savings: $2,380,000–$3,570,000 per qualifying shareholder**

## Does Gumroad Qualify?

Walking through each QSBS requirement:

### 1. Domestic C-Corporation

**Status: PASS**

Gumroad, Inc. is a Delaware C-Corporation and has been since incorporation in 2011. It has never made an S-Corp election.

### 2. Original Issuance Requirement

**Status: PASS (for Sahil and original investors)**

QSBS must be acquired at original issuance -- meaning directly from the corporation, not purchased on a secondary market. Sahil's founder stock was issued directly by Gumroad. The same is true for shares issued to seed investors, Series A participants, option holders who exercise, and Crowd SAFE holders upon conversion.

Secondary purchases (e.g., if someone bought shares from an existing investor) would not qualify.

### 3. Gross Assets Test ($50M/$75M)

**Status: NEEDS VERIFICATION -- but likely PASS at issuance**

At the time Sahil's stock was issued (2011), Gumroad's gross assets were minimal -- essentially zero. The test requires that gross assets did not exceed $50M at the time of issuance **and at all times before and immediately after** issuance.

Key question: Did Gumroad's gross assets ever exceed $50M? At a $100-125M valuation, this depends on what counts as "gross assets." The IRS uses the aggregate adjusted basis of corporate assets (cash + tax basis of other assets), not fair market value. For a capital-light software company like Gumroad, the tax basis of assets is likely well below $50M even today.

**This must be verified with actual financial statements.** If gross assets exceeded $50M at the time any particular stock was issued, that tranche of stock would not qualify.

### 4. Active Business Requirement (80% Test)

**Status: LIKELY PASS**

At least 80% of Gumroad's assets (by value) must be used in the active conduct of a qualified trade or business. Gumroad is an operating software platform -- its assets are primarily cash, receivables, and technology infrastructure, all used in the active business.

The main risk here would be if Gumroad held large amounts of passive investments or real estate. Based on publicly available information, this does not appear to be the case.

### 5. Qualified Trade or Business

**Status: PASS**

Section 1202 excludes certain professional service businesses (health, law, engineering, accounting, actuarial science, performing arts, consulting, athletics, financial services, brokerage). Technology and software businesses are explicitly eligible.

Gumroad is a software platform. It qualifies.

### 6. Holding Period (5 Years)

**Status: PASS for Sahil; TIME-SENSITIVE for crowdfunding investors**

- **Sahil's founder stock:** Issued 2011. Held for 15 years. Easily meets the 5-year requirement.
- **Seed and Series A investors:** Stock issued 2012. Held for 14 years. Easily qualifies (for those still holding).
- **Crowd SAFE holders (March 2021):** This is where it gets complicated.

## The Crowdfunding Investor QSBS Question

The 7,387 investors from the March 2021 Republic raise hold Crowd SAFEs -- not equity. The QSBS holding period generally begins when the taxpayer acquires **stock**, not when they acquire a right to stock.

**If the SAFEs have not yet converted to equity:** The 5-year clock has not started. These investors do not yet hold QSBS.

**If the SAFEs convert to equity:** The 5-year clock starts at conversion. If conversion happens in 2026, the earliest these investors could sell with QSBS benefits would be 2031.

**If the SAFEs are treated as equity from inception** (an aggressive but arguable position under certain circumstances): The 5-year mark is March 2026 -- right now.

This is a time-sensitive issue that affects 7,387 people. The correct treatment depends on:
- The specific terms of the Crowd SAFE agreement
- Whether a triggering event has occurred
- How the IRS treats SAFEs for Section 1202 purposes (guidance is limited)
- Whether Gumroad meets all QSBS requirements at the time of conversion

**Action required:** Legal and tax counsel should analyze the SAFE terms and provide guidance to investors. If QSBS is available, this is extraordinarily valuable information that Gumroad's investors need to know.

## Why S-Corp Conversion Would Be Catastrophic

**Converting Gumroad to an S-Corp would permanently destroy QSBS eligibility for all shareholders.**

Here is why:

1. **Section 1202 requires C-Corp status.** QSBS must be stock of a C-Corporation. The moment Gumroad elects S-Corp status, all outstanding stock ceases to be QSBS.

2. **There is no grandfathering.** Stock that was QSBS before the S-Corp election does not retain its QSBS status. The conversion retroactively disqualifies the stock.

3. **The damage is permanent.** Even if Gumroad later revoked its S-Corp election and returned to C-Corp status, the QSBS clock would have to restart from zero. For Sahil, that means waiting another 5 years -- and losing the benefit of 15 years of holding.

4. **The dollar value at stake.** $2.38M–$3.57M for Sahil alone (depending on OBBBA applicability). Potentially millions more across all qualifying shareholders.

No reasonable S-Corp tax savings could ever justify destroying $2.38M–$3.57M+ in QSBS benefits.
